# Cold Email Writing Framework
*Distilled from "30 Scripts That Won" by Christian Bonnier — 46 proven templates across industries*

---

## Anatomy of a winning email

4 components in order:
1. **Hook** — observation, pain question, or bold claim (1 sentence)
2. **Offer** — what you do + specific outcome (1-2 sentences)
3. **Proof** — specific metric or named client result (1 sentence)
4. **CTA** — single soft ask (1 sentence, never a calendar link)

Total: 50-85 words. 2-4 short paragraphs. Never more.

---

## Subject lines

**Rules:**
- 2-5 words max. Lowercase. No punctuation except `?`
- Never: "Following up", "Re:", "Introduction", "Partnership opportunity"

**5 subject line types that convert:**

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Specific outcome | `cut dealer ramp-up 55%` |
| Named thing | `{{company_name}} on Amazon` |
| Curiosity question | `looking for minute misers?` |
| Problem framing | `eliminate plaster callbacks` |
| Neutral opener | `quick question` (use sparingly — only when body is very strong) |

---

## Hook patterns (opening line)

Pick one per email. Never repeat the same pattern in a sequence.

**1. Observation hook** — mention something verifiable about the company
> "I came across your store on Amazon and had a few ideas to increase sales."
> "I searched for {{company_name}} on Google and noticed a few competitors outranking you."

**2. Pain question hook** — ask directly about a known friction point
> "Struggling with slow sales growth or high ACoS on Amazon?"
> "How fast is your team following up with new leads?"

**3. Bold claim hook** — lead with the result, no buildup
> "We will add $40k to $200k to your monthly revenue in 60 days or less using email, or you get a complete refund."
> "Most roofs fail in hurricane-force winds and insurance often won't cover."

**4. Role acknowledgment hook** — reference their specific position
> "As a decision maker at {{company_name}}, you need more qualified pipeline."
> "Figured you might oversee new jobs at {{company_name}}."

**Rules:**
- Personalized first line must include 1 specific verifiable detail (metric, launch, job post, event, ranking, funding)
- 8-22 words
- No "I", no flattery, no vague observations ("I noticed you're doing great things")

## Offer framing

- State what you do in plain language — no jargon
- Anchor to outcome, not features
- Use "we help [ICP] do [outcome]" or "we [action] so [result]"

**Good:**
> "We specialize in SaaS SEO that converts existing content into SQL-generating assets."
> "We provide skilled, pre-vetted real estate SDRs to handle outreach and follow-ups so you can focus on client experience."

**Bad:**
> "We offer a comprehensive suite of demand generation solutions designed to optimize your go-to-market motion."

---

## Social proof

Always specific. Never vague.

**Formula:** `[client descriptor] + [specific result] + [timeframe]`

> "We recently helped a client lift SQLs 30% in 90 days."
> "David generated 537+ ACA policies in under 30 days."
> "A contractor increased average project value from $25k to $250k by switching from wood to stone."
> "A similar company boosted close rate from 27% to 64%, doubling revenue in two years."

**Rules:**
- Name the client or describe them specifically ("a Buffalo-based roofing company", "a healthcare SaaS founder")
- Include a number: %, $, days, count — always
- One proof point per email. Don't stack multiple.

---

## CTAs

Single sentence. Question format. Low commitment. No calendar links in Email 1 or 2.

**Hierarchy (softest to hardest):**
1. "Mind if I share [X]?" — softest, highest reply rate
2. "Can I send a quick video?" — creates curiosity
3. "Open to [specific outcome]?" — slightly more direct
4. "Worth exploring?" — neutral, works when proof is strong
5. "Would you like to schedule a call this week?" — only in Email 3 (break-up)

**What "X" can be:** a short video, a Loom, a PDF, a few ideas, how it works

**Never use:** "Let's chat", "Book a 30-minute call", "Schedule time on my calendar", "Would love to connect"

## The 3-email sequence

### Email 1 (Day 0) — Personalized opener
- Use the research: specific verifiable hook about their company
- Lead with strongest proof point
- Softest CTA (video, ideas, Loom)
- 60-80 words

### Email 2 (Day 3-4) — Different angle
- New hook, new pain point — don't repeat Email 1
- Option: numbered list format ("Two quick things: 1)... 2)...")
- Option: rhetorical question opener, then pivot to offer
- Lead with a different proof point or result
- Slightly softer CTA than Email 1
- 50-70 words

### Email 3 (Day 7-10) — Break-up
- 2-3 sentences only
- Open: "One last note —" or "Reaching out one last time."
- No pressure framing: "if [X] isn't a priority right now, no worries"
- Close with a direct ask: "Would you like to schedule a call this week?"
- 30-45 words

## A/B Variants (Email 1 rewrites)

### Variant A — Before/After/Bridge
Structure: Pain state (where they are now) → Transformed state (where they want to be) → Bridge (how you get them there)

> Before: "Most compliance SaaS founders are stuck on referrals — unpredictable, hard to scale."
> After: "3+ qualified meetings a month, running in the background, no events required."
> Bridge: "We built the outbound system that makes that happen."

### Variant B — Curiosity + Value
Structure: Intriguing hook that withholds information → specific value statement → soft ask to learn more

> "Here's something most compliance SaaS founders don't know: the right cold email system can out-perform an SDR in your first 30 days — without the $6K/month headcount cost."

### Variant C — Direct social proof
Structure: Named result up front → direct offer → close

> "We've booked 3+ qualified meetings in 30 days for compliance and safety SaaS founders, consistently, across multiple clients in adjacent spaces. Want to see if [company] is a fit?"

## What kills cold emails

- Subject lines over 6 words
- Opening with "I" ("I wanted to reach out", "I hope this finds you well")
- Vague hooks ("I noticed you're doing some impressive work")
- Feature lists instead of outcomes
- Multiple CTAs in one email
- Asking for a 30-minute call in Email 1
- Generic proof ("we've helped companies like yours")
- Em dashes
- Filler phrases: "I'd love to", "I'd be happy to", "Just wanted to"
